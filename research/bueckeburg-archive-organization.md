# Bückeburg Archive: Organization of Holdings

The organization of the Bückeburg regional archive is along these lines.

For a particular archival file's identifier, say, for example, 
**NLA BU, L 2, L Nr.  27/28**, the breakdown of each segment is as follows:

**NLA BU**
**NLA BU** = **Niedersächisches Landesarchiv, Abteilung Bückeburg** — the
Bückeburg regional department of the Lower Saxony State Archives.

**L**
**L** is an **Abteilung** (division), a top-level archival division within
the Bückeburg archive, that contains Schaumburg-Lippe governmental records.

**L 2**
**L 2** is the **Bestand**. In English, you can think of it as the
**fonds** **Fonds** is the boyd of records from a particular
creator-entity. It is the specific fonds-level body of records within the
larger **L** division.

**L Nr. 27/28**
This is the specific file or item reference. It is part of the
**Signatur**, meaning the archival call number or reference code for the
document.

**Gliederung**
Gliederung means the **hierarchival arrangement**.

**NLA BU** is the archive department.
Inside NLA BU is the top-level holdings division **L**.
Inside **L** is the fonds / record group **L 2**.
Inside **L 2** is the specific file or item **L Nr. 27/28**.


## Top-Level Holding Divisions

According to [Lower Saxony State Archives, Bückeburg Branch](https://www.arcinsys.niedersachsen.de/arcinsys/detailAction.action?detailid=a2),
its holdings are subdivided into these top-level divisions:

Orig. = Documents
L = Authorities of the old County of Schaumburg (until 1647) and Schaumburg-Lippe
H = Authorities of the Hessian part of the County of Schaumburg or of the Rinteln district/County of Schaumburg
D = Estates and private archives transferred to the State Archives
E = Minor acquisitions
S = Collections (maps, photos, microfiches of church registers, microfiches of newspapers, etc.);
Dep. = Deposits (private archives entrusted to the State Archives for safekeeping and use), including:
- Princely Schaumburg-Lippe House Archives
- Schaumburg-Lippe Court Chamber Archives
- Estate and family archives, e.g., von Münchhausen
- Companies of the private sector, e.g., Obernkirchen Mining Authority / Hanover Chamber of Industry and Commerce, Stadthagen branch
- Church archives, e.g., Obernkirchen Abbey, Schaumburg-Lippe State Church;
- Municipal archives, e.g., B. Schaumburg district, towns of Bückeburg / Hessisch Oldendorf / Obernkirchen / Rodenberg / Sachsenhagen

The most relevant to my genealogical research is **L**, which is
governing authorities of the old County of Schaumburg and Schaumburg-Lippe.


## Discovering the Bestände/Fonds

Use the hierarchy like this:

**1. Start with the right top-level division.**
For your ancestors, that is probably **L**, because it contains Schaumburg-Lippe governmental records.

**2. Within L, identify the relevant Bestand/fonds.**
For Berenbusch, Petzen, and Evesen, look especially for Bestände connected with **Amt Bückeburg**.

**3. Within that Bestand, look for files by place and record type.**
Do not rely mainly on surname searches.

Search or browse for:

**Places:**
Berenbusch / Bärenbusch / Bernbusch, Petzen, Evesen

**Record types:**
tax lists, householder lists, land surveys, visitations, residence records, trade permissions, petitions

**Occupations/status words:**
Schneider, Schuster, Schuhmacher, Leineweber, Einlieger, Inwohner, Handwerker, Gewerbe

The key point:

> **The Bestand tells you which office created the records. Once you find the right office, search its files for places and record types likely to name ordinary residents.**
