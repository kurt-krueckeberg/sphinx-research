# Prospective Records for Tracking Holdings in Berenbusch

Prospective tecords in the NLA Bückeburg archives containing

- Haus- und Hofbesitzer lists
- Landesvermessung records
- tax registers
- contribution registers
- service-obligation records
- forest/allocation records
- sale or transfer records
- and later Grundbuch / land-register material.

## Highest-priority records

**1. NLA BU, L 2, L Nr. 27/54** **Spezialvermessung der Dörfer Evesen,
Berenbusch und Nordholz**, 1734–1742.  This is probably the most important
companion to the 1743–1744 material. It is explicitly for **Evesen,
Berenbusch, and Nordholz** and should be checked for house/holding numbers,
land descriptions, measurements, owners/holders, and possibly earlier names
attached to holdings. ([Arcinsys][1])

**2. NLA BU, L 2, L Nr. 27/13** **Vermessung der Dörfer Evesen, Berenbusch
und Nordholz**, 1745.  This looks like the next chronological measurement
record after the 1734–1742 Spezialvermessung. It should be compared line by
line with L Nr. 27/54 and the 1743–1744 Hofbesitzer interrogation/protocol
material. ([Arcinsys][2])

**3. NLA BU, L 2, L Nr. 27/28** **Erfassung der Haus- und Hofbesitzer im
Amt Bückeburg im Rahmen einer Generallandesvisitation**, 1737.  This is the
broader 1737 Amt Bückeburg house-and-farm-holder register. It may not be as
detailed for Berenbusch as the village-specific files, but it is directly
relevant because it identifies **Haus- und Hofbesitzer** in the Amt
Bückeburg. ([Arcinsys][3])

**4. NLA BU, L 31, Nr. 12** **Berenbusch: Verzeichnis der Pflichtigen,
ihrer Gefälle und Ablösungsbeträge, auch Einzelverträge**, 1860–1873.  This
is a major target. It should list the persons liable for dues, the
payments/obligations being redeemed, redemption amounts, and individual
contracts. For a holding history, this is likely more useful than ordinary
church records because it ties people to fiscal burdens and holdings.
([Arcinsys][4])

## Later but still useful records

**5. NLA BU, L 102a, Nr. 529** **Berenbusch-Nordholz: Abtrennungen und
Zulegungen von Grundstücken**, 1880–1918.  This concerns separations and
additions of parcels. It is later than your main period, but it may
preserve continuity of parcel ownership, house numbers, or references back
to older land-register data. ([Arcinsys][5])

**6. NLA BU, L 102a, Nr. 531** **Berenbusch-Nordholz:
Grundbuchanlageverfahren**, 1908–1935.  This is very important for the
later land-register setup. It will not solve the 1740s by itself, but it
may contain the transition into the modern Grundbuch and may cite older
ownership or parcel data. ([Arcinsys][5])

**7. NLA BU, L 102a, Nr. 535** **Berenbusch-Nordholz: Geschlossene
Bauernhöfe und das Anerbenrecht; enthält Höferegister**, 1910.  This is
late, but a **Höferegister** can be useful because it may list recognized
closed farms and their status, sometimes with house numbers or owners.
([Arcinsys][5])

**8. NLA BU, L 102a, Nr. 536** **Heinrich Weiland, Großköter in Berenbusch
Nr. 1**, 1908–1933.  This is an example of the kind of individual holding
file in L 102a. Arcinsys shows Berenbusch No. 1; there may be additional
Berenbusch-numbered files beyond page 1 of the Berenbusch-Nordholz list.
You would want to inspect the full L 102a Berenbusch-Nordholz
classification, not just the first page. ([Arcinsys][5])

## Useful but secondary

**9. NLA BU, L 101a, Kc Nr. 2** **Zwei abgängig gewordene Kämpe im gemeinen
Wege zu Nordholz und Berenbusch, desgleichen vom Wöbkinger Hof Nr. 7 zur
Bauernschaft Röcke**, 1789.  This is not a general holding-history record,
but it involves land/common-way issues in Nordholz and Berenbusch and names
a numbered Hof. It could help with location and neighboring-property
context. ([Arcinsys][6])

**10. NLA BU, L 101a, Kc Nr. 11** **Brücken- und Wegebesserungen in Evesen,
Nordholz und Berenbusch**, 1804–1827.  Again, not a direct ownership file,
but road/bridge matters can name obligated villagers, adjacent holdings, or
land affected by public works. ([Arcinsys][7])

**11. NLA BU, L 102a, Nr. 520** **Berenbusch-Nordholz:
Gemeindeangelegenheiten; includes petitions concerning Wegerechte,
Wasserrechte, Armenrecht**, 1911–1936.  Late, but **Wegerechte** and
**Wasserrechte** can preserve property relationships, easements, and
disputes. ([Arcinsys][5])

**12. NLA BU, L 32, I Nr. 10** **Evesen - Berenbusch - Nordholz;
Erbhofakten**, 1931–1948.  This is Nazi-era Erbhof material, not
18th-century evidence. But Erbhof files can include farm status, owners,
family succession, and sometimes older supporting extracts. Use it only as
a backward clue, not as primary evidence for the 1700s. ([Arcinsys][8])

**13. NLA BU, L 32, II Nr. 6.1 and Nr. 6.2** **Enzen - Hobbensen - Evesen -
Berenbusch - Nordholz - Frille; Gemeindeakten / Höfe**, 1933–1949.  Same
caution: late material, but it may contain farm files or administrative
data for named Höfe in the combined area. ([Arcinsys][9])

## What I would ask the archive for

Ask specifically for a search under **Berenbusch Nr. 10** and **Berenbusch
Nr. 18** in these record groups:

**L 2** — Landesvermessung / Spezialvermessung / Haus- und Hofbesitzer **L
31** — Ablösungsverfahren, especially Berenbusch Nr. 12 **L 101a** —
Gemeinde- and Wegeakten for Berenbusch/Nordholz/Evesen **L 102a** —
Landratsamt Bückeburg, Berenbusch-Nordholz, especially Grundstücke,
Grundbuchanlageverfahren, Höferegister, and individual holding files **L
32** — Erbhofakten and Gemeindeakten, only for late corroborative clues

The most concrete next step is **not** a broad search for “Krückeberg.” It
is to order or inspect **L 2 L Nr. 27/54**, **L 2 L Nr. 27/13**, **L 2 L
Nr. 27/28**, and **L 31 Nr. 12** first. Those are the core files most
likely to bridge the 1730s–1870s holding history.
