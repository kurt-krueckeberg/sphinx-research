# Are Einlieger Listed Anyway for 1725-1745?

For a **Stätte holder**, you have a trail: house number, land survey, tax
lists, sale records, inheritance files, forest allotments, etc.

For an **Einlieger**, the person may leave almost no paper trail unless
some administrative event forced him into the records: tax liability, poor
relief, military conscription, permission to reside, marriage approval,
debt, guardianship, service, emigration, or an attempt to establish a new
Stätte.

So the realistic strategy is not “search every possible file.” It is:

**1. First ask whether true population/non-holder lists survive.** That
means Einliegerverzeichnisse, Einwohnerverzeichnisse, Beisitzerlisten,
Seelenregister, Schutzgeldregister, Konskriptionslisten, Armenregister. If
none survive for Amt Bückeburg/Petzen/Berenbusch, then you know the absence
of such a list is itself the limitation.

**2. Use the church registers as the spine.** Extract every
Krückeberg/Kruckeberg/Krickeberg entry in Petzen, including sponsors,
occupations, residences, marriage witnesses, burial descriptors, and
phrases like *Schneider*, *Einlieger*, *Beisitzer*, *in Berenbusch*, *bei
Nr. X*.

**3. Correlate only with targeted civil/administrative record types.** Do
not transliterate broad files unless the title strongly indicates names of
ordinary residents. The high-value categories are: conscription, poor
relief, protection/residence money, tax registers naming individuals,
guardianship, estate/debt matters, and permissions to establish a new
holding.

**4. Treat “only Krückebergs at no. 10” as a hypothesis, not a
conclusion.** You can strengthen it if repeated non-church sources show
only no. 10 Krückebergs and no separate Einlieger Krückebergs. But without
an Einwohner/Einlieger list, you may not be able to prove a negative.

The best immediate move is therefore not more Arcinsys guessing. It is a
precise archive question: **Do population or non-holder lists survive for
Amt Bückeburg, especially Berenbusch/Evesen/Nordholz/Petzen, ca.
1720–1850?** If the archivist says no, that saves you months.
