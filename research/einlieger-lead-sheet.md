# Berenbusch and Petzen Parish Einlieger and Non-Holderw Research Leads, 1725–1775

## Scope

Target:

```text
Krückeberg/Kruckeberg persons in Berenbusch, Petzen parish, or rural Amt Bückeburg
who may have been Einlieger, Häuslinge, non-holding residents, rural craftsmen,
lodgers, servants, or otherwise not holders of a Stätte.
```

Excluded:

```text
- Stadt Bückeburg
- Stadtgericht Bückeburg
- Bürger records
- town Einlieger / Schutzgeld records
- Dep. 9 A Stadt Bückeburg records
- your own nla.krueckeberg.org / docs.krueckeberg.org work
- known Berenbusch land-survey / Hofbesitzer records
```

## Main finding

After removing Stadt Bückeburg, I did **not** find a clearly identified, order-ready **rural Amt Bückeburg Einliegerverzeichnis / Häuslingsverzeichnis for 1725–1775** in the accessible Arcinsys results.

The only directly relevant rural headings I could verify are classification headings, not confirmed usable files from your date range.

---

## 1. K 1 > Amt Bückeburg > 5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge

**Status:** best rural lead, but not yet proven by item-level evidence.

Arcinsys shows **NLA BU > K 1 > Amt Bückeburg** and the classification heading **“5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge.”** However, the visible item list under that Amt Bückeburg node is dominated by **geäußerte Höfe** and holder-centered files, not clear Einlieger/Häuslinge lists from 1725–1775. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Assessment:**
This is the one to ask the archive about, but I would **not order blindly** unless they confirm there are actual rural files under 5.3 for the period.

**Archive question:**

```text
Does K 1 > Amt Bückeburg > 5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge contain any files
from ca. 1725–1775 that cover the rural villages of Amt Bückeburg,
especially Berenbusch or Petzen parish?
```

---

## 2. K 2 > Aktiva im Amt Bückeburg > 10.20 Einlieger oder Häuslinge

**Status:** relevant category, but apparently too late for your problem.

Arcinsys shows **K 2 > Aktiva im Amt Bückeburg** with the heading **“10.20 Einlieger oder Häuslinge.”** The visible examples, however, are mostly 19th-century capital files; the explicitly rural Einlieger example I found is **Einlieger Engelking Nr. 8 in Südhorsten**, dated **1862–1866**. ([Arcinsys][2])

**Assessment:**
Do **not** make this a 1725–1775 priority unless the archive confirms earlier hidden or unlisted files under the same classification.

---

## 3. Rural personal-tax / head-tax records

**Status:** conceptually strong, item not yet found.

For Einlieger, the better tax route would be **person-based taxation**, not holding-based taxation. Useful terms:

```text
Kopfsteuer
Kopfschatz
Kopfgeld
Personalsteuer
Schatzung
Kontribution, if person-based rather than holding-based
```

**Assessment:**
This is a good archive-question category, but I did not find a clean rural Amt Bückeburg item for 1725–1775 in the accessible search results.

**Archive question:**

```text
Are there Kopfsteuer-, Kopfschatz-, Personalsteuer-, or other person-based
tax registers for rural Amt Bückeburg, ca. 1725–1775, that name non-holding
residents, Einlieger, Häuslinge, servants, or craftsmen by village?
```

## 5. Rural Schutzgeld / Einliegergeld / Schützlinge records

**Status:** strong in theory, not confirmed for rural Amt Bückeburg.

This would be excellent if it exists for the rural Amt, because such records may name non-holding protected residents or lodgers.

Search/ask terms:

```text
Schutzgeld
Einliegergeld
Schützlinge
Beisassen
Aufnahme von Einliegern
Ausweisung von Einliegern
```

**Assessment:**
Do not use the Stadt Bückeburg Schutzgeld records. Ask specifically whether there are **rural Amt Bückeburg** Schutzgeld/Einliegergeld records.
