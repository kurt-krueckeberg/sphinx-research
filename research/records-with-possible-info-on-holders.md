## New or still-worth-checking leads

### 1. **NLA BU K 1 D Nr. 143 — Dienstpflichtigen-register for Amt Bückeburg**

**Title:** *Die von den Dienstpflichtigen des Amts Bückeburg zu leistenden Hand- und Spanndienste, auch Burgfeste und Erntetage und davon angefertigte Register*
**Date range:** **1667–1730**

This is the best new lead I found. It is not a Berenbusch-title hit; it is an **Amt-wide service-obligation register**. Because your Berenbusch Krückeberg household owed hand services, Burgfeste, and Erntetage in the 1740s, this record type is directly relevant. Its weakness is that it ends in 1730, so it will not identify the 1750 baptism father directly, but it could identify the Krückeberg household around the 1727 marriage period. ([Arcinsys][1])

### 2. **NLA BU L 2 C Nr. 2a — Kirchspiele und Namen der Hauswirte**

**Title:** *Kirchspiele und Namen der Hauswirte*
**Date range:** **1731–1748**

This remains a serious lead because it is organized by **parishes** and **names of householders**, not just village land-survey files. It may still miss Einlieger/Häuslinge, but if the “Leineweber Krückeberg” was treated as a Hauswirt before 1750, this is one of the few records that could bridge church-register identities with household status. ([Arcinsys][2])

### 3. **NLA BU L 2 P Nr. 8 — Populationstabellen der Grafschaft Schaumburg-Lippe**

**Title:** *Populationstabellen der Grafschaft Schaumburg-Lippe*
**Date:** **1732**
**Contains:** Arcinsys notes named recording of farmers by rural possession classes.

This is worth checking, but with a clear limitation: it may capture **bäuerliche Besitzklassen**, not a landless or semi-landless linen weaver. Still, it is not merely a Berenbusch title hit and could help determine whether there was a Krückeberg household in the relevant parish/office structure in 1732. ([Arcinsys][2])

### 4. **NLA BU K 1 E Nr. 32 — Accounts of “geäußerte Höfe” in Ämter Bückeburg and Arensburg**

**Title:** *Die von den geäußerten Höfen in den Ämtern Bückeburg und Arensburg geführten Rechnungen und deren justification*
**Date range:** **1708–1774**

This is a better lead than random debt files. It covers **geäußerte Höfe** across Amt Bückeburg and Arensburg and spans your entire period. It is relevant because “Äußerung” was an administrative/debt-management context for troubled holdings, and such accounts may name holders, lessees, or responsible householders. It is not guaranteed to include Berenbusch No. 10, but it is a real series-level target, not a surname/village title search. ([Arcinsys][3])

### 5. **NLA BU L 2 L Nr. 11 — Organisation of the Generallandesvisitation and Lagerbücher**

**Title:** *Organisation einer Generallandesvisitation und Maßnahmen zur Anlegung flächendeckener Lagerbücher*
**Date range:** **1731–1742**

This is not a first-order parent-identification source, but it may explain what ancillary lists, instructions, or working papers were created for the visitation/Lagerbuch project. I would treat it as a **context-and-finding-aid lead**, not as a file likely to name Carl Philip’s parents. ([Arcinsys][2])

### 6. **NLA BU L 2 L Nr. 27/25 and 27/26 — preparation/supervision files for the Landesvisitation**

**Titles:**
`L 2 L Nr. 27/25` — *Vorbereitung einer Generallandesvisitation (Landesvermessung)*, **1728–1732**
`L 2 L Nr. 27/26` — *Aufsicht über eine Generallandesvisitation (Landesvermessung)*, **1741–1750**

These are secondary leads. They may contain correspondence, schedules, complaints, or attached lists that did not end up in the village files. I would not order them before K 1 D Nr. 143 or L 2 C Nr. 2a, but they are legitimate follow-up leads because the second one reaches **1750**, the year of Carl Philip’s baptism. ([Arcinsys][2])

## What I would drop or de-prioritize

I did **not** find a good Amt Bückeburg **Eheberedungen** series for 1725–1765. The archive/publication information I found points to **Amt Stadthagen** Eheberedungen, not Amt Bückeburg/Petzen. So I would not make Eheberedungen a current lead unless the archive itself identifies a hidden Amt Bückeburg series. ([Arcinsys][4])

I also did **not** find a useful 1725–1765 Amt Bückeburg guardianship/probate series. The guardianship-like results I found were later and in the Rinteln judicial context, so they are not good leads for your Berenbusch/Petzen problem. ([Arcinsys][5])

I would also drop **Leineweber/Weberamt** as a primary route. The relevant hits I found were much later or urban/judicial, such as a Bückeburg Stadtgericht record from 1823–1824 and a Rinteln Ehebeschreibung from 1850, not rural Amt Bückeburg around 1750. ([Arcinsys][6])

## Corrected priority order

```text
1. NLA BU K 1 D Nr. 143 — Amt-wide Dienstpflichtigen registers, 1667–1730
2. NLA BU L 2 C Nr. 2a — Kirchspiele und Namen der Hauswirte, 1731–1748
3. NLA BU L 2 P Nr. 8 — Populationstabellen, 1732
4. NLA BU K 1 E Nr. 32 — geäußerte Höfe accounts, 1708–1774
5. NLA BU L 2 L Nr. 27/26 — supervision of Landesvisitation, 1741–1750
6. NLA BU L 2 L Nr. 11 / 27/25 — visitation/Lagerbuch organization files
```

The strongest genuinely new lead is **K 1 D Nr. 143**. The strongest possible parent-identification lead is probably **L 2 C Nr. 2a**, because it is explicitly about **parishes and names of householders** rather than Berenbusch land parcels.
