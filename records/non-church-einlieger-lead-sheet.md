# Non-church archival leads for possible Krückeberg Einlieger, 1725–1775

This exlucdes nla.krueckeberg.org documents and documents for
Stätte-holders/land-survey

## Scope

Target: **Krückeberg/Kruckeberg persons in or near Berenbusch / Petzen parish who may have been Einlieger, Häuslinge, non-holding lodgers, protected residents, servants, craftsmen, or other non-Stätte holders.**

Excluded from this list:

```text
- nla.krueckeberg.org / docs.krueckeberg.org
- the known Berenbusch / Petzen land-survey and Hofbesitzer records
- records useful mainly for Stätte holders
- mere surname-title or Berenbusch-title searches
```

## Main conclusion

I found **far fewer strong rural Einlieger leads** than Stätte-holder leads.

For **Berenbusch / Petzen parish specifically**, I did **not** find a clearly titled **Amt Bückeburg Einliegerverzeichnis** or **Häuslingsverzeichnis** for **1725–1775** in the accessible Arcinsys results.

The strongest true Einlieger records I found are for **Stadt Bückeburg**, not the rural villages. They are still worth knowing about, but they are not direct substitutes for Berenbusch/Petzen village Einlieger lists.

---

## A. Highest-value Einlieger leads found, but mostly Stadt Bückeburg

### 1. NLA BU, Dep. 9 A, Nr. 747

**Title:** *Einlieger und Visitation derselben Band 1*
**Date range:** **1624–1738**
**Relevance:** Strong Einlieger record type, but for **Stadt Bückeburg**, not rural Berenbusch. Arcinsys says it includes **Einliegerverzeichnisse**, including lists from 1645 and 1691ff. It overlaps the early part of your target period through 1738. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Medium. It could identify a Krückeberg if one was resident in **Bückeburg town** before 1738, but it is not a direct Berenbusch/Petzen source.

---

### 2. NLA BU, Dep. 9 A, Nr. 748

**Title:** *Erfassung der Einlieger in Bückeburg und Visitation derselben*
**Date range:** **1738–1805**
**Relevance:** Strong Einlieger record type. It directly covers the period around the **1750 baptism** and continues well beyond 1775. But again, it is **Bückeburg town**, not the rural Amt villages. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Medium to high **only if** a Krückeberg moved into Bückeburg town, worked there, lodged there, or appears as a protected resident. For Berenbusch itself, it is not a primary source.

---

### 3. NLA BU, Dep. 9 A, Nr. 750

**Title:** *Betreffend Ausweisung von Einliegern*
**Date range:** **1711–1755**
**Relevance:** This covers your period and concerns the **expulsion of Einlieger**. Likely Stadt Bückeburg jurisdiction because it is in Dep. 9 A, Stadt Bückeburg. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Medium. More likely to name problematic or unwanted Einlieger than all ordinary Einlieger. It could be useful if a Krückeberg was a mobile or marginal resident, but it is not a general population list.

---

### 4. NLA BU, Dep. 9 A, Nr. 751

**Title:** *Visitationen zwecks Feststellung von Vagabunden in Bückeburg und deren Ausweisung*
**Date range:** **1722–1789**
**Relevance:** Covers the whole target period. It is about inspections for identifying vagrants and expelling them; Arcinsys notes named mentions, especially of Jews and their places of residence. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Low to medium. This is not a normal Einlieger register, but it may name non-settled or marginal residents. I would not put it first unless you are expanding beyond stable Petzen parish residents.

---

### 5. NLA BU, Dep. 9 A, Nr. 755

**Title:** *Ansatz des Schutzgeldes der Einlieger in der Stadt Bückeburg jährliche Berichte an die Regierung mit dem Verzeichnis der vorhandenen Schützlinge …*
**Date range:** **1767–1805**
**Relevance:** Very good record type: annual reports with a register/list of protected residents or Schützlinge. But it begins **after 1765** and concerns **Stadt Bückeburg**. ([Arcinsys][1])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Medium. It is later than the 1750 baptism but within your expanded **to 1775** range. Worth checking if a Krückeberg appears in Bückeburg town after the mid-century period.

---

## B. Conceptually promising, but not yet proven useful for rural Berenbusch/Petzen

### 6. K 1, Amt Bückeburg — classification “5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge”

Arcinsys search results show that **K 1 > Amt Bückeburg** includes the classification heading **“5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge.”** That is exactly the right conceptual category. But the accessible visible item list I found under K 1 did not show a clear 1725–1775 rural Einlieger/Häusling register; the visible records were mostly **geäußerte Höfe** and holder-centered files. ([Arcinsys][2])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Potentially high, but not verified. This is the classification I would ask the archive about directly:

```text
Does K 1, Amt Bückeburg, 5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge contain any files or lists from ca. 1725–1775 that cover Berenbusch, Petzen parish, or the rural Amt Bückeburg villages?
```

This is probably the most important archival follow-up question from this search.

---

### 7. NLA BU L 2 P Nr. 8 — Populationstabellen, 1732

This is not an Einlieger register. Arcinsys says it includes **named recording of Bauern by possession classes** and **numerical recording of other household residents**. That means Einlieger or other non-holding residents may be counted but probably **not named**. ([Arcinsys][3])

**Usefulness for Einlieger research:**
Low for names, useful for context. It may show the existence or number of non-holding residents, but probably not identify a Krückeberg Einlieger by name.

---

## C. Useful analogues, but wrong place or wrong period

### 8. NLA BU L 2 S Nr. 40 — Stadthagen Neubürger, Schützlinge, Einlieger

**Title:** *Erfassung von Neubürgern, Schützlingen und Einliegern in Stadthagen*
**Date range:** **1610–1777**
**Relevance:** Excellent record type, but it concerns **Stadthagen**, not Bückeburg/Petzen/Berenbusch. It includes short lists with names. ([Arcinsys][4])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Low unless you are tracing wider Krückeberg mobility. Its value is mainly methodological: it proves that this kind of named Einlieger/Schützling record existed in Schaumburg-Lippe administration.

---

### 9. L 1 early Einlieger and Kopfsteuer records

I found earlier examples of exactly the kind of records that would be useful, but they are much too early and/or wrong Amt: for example, **Verzeichnis der Einlieger im Amt** from **1570** and a **Kopfsteuerregister** from **1552** that includes names with women, children, and servants. ([Arcinsys][5])

**Usefulness for your problem:**
Not directly useful for 1725–1775. But they show the kinds of terms to use when asking the archive:

```text
Einliegerverzeichnis
Kopfsteuerregister
Kopfschatzregister
Schutzgeldregister
Schützlingsverzeichnis
Namensliste mit Frauen, Kindern und Gesinde
```

## D. What I would actually order or ask about

I would **not** order all of these blindly. I would first ask the archive a focused question.

### Best archive question

```text
I am looking for non-church records naming Einlieger, Häuslinge, Schützlinge, lodgers, servants, or other non-holding residents in the rural Amt Bückeburg, especially Berenbusch and Petzen parish, ca. 1725–1775.

Arcinsys shows K 1 > Amt Bückeburg with the classification “5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge.” Do any files under this classification survive for ca. 1725–1775, and do they cover the rural villages of Amt Bückeburg, including Berenbusch/Petzen parish?

I am not asking for Stätte-holder, Haus- und Hofbesitzer, or Landesvermessung records, but specifically for records likely to name non-holding residents.
```

### If they say yes, this is the best path

```text
1. K 1 > Amt Bückeburg > 5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge
   Priority: highest, if there are files from 1725–1775.

2. Any Amt Bückeburg Schutzgeld / Einliegergeld / Schützlinge records
   Priority: high, if rural Amt-wide rather than Stadt Bückeburg only.

3. Any Kopfsteuer / Kopfschatz / person-tax records for Amt Bückeburg, 1725–1775
   Priority: high, because these are more likely than land records to capture non-holding people.

4. Any Einwohner-, Untertanen-, Haushalts-, or Gesinde lists for Amt Bückeburg, 1725–1775
   Priority: medium to high.

5. Stadt Bückeburg Dep. 9 A Einlieger records
   Priority: secondary, useful only for Krückebergs who may have moved into town.
```

## Final assessment

For **Einlieger**, the direct archival lead is weaker than for Stätte holders.

The best confirmed records are **Stadt Bückeburg Einlieger/Schutzgeld files**, especially **Dep. 9 A Nr. 747, 748, 750, 751, and 755**. The best unconfirmed but potentially decisive rural lead is **K 1 > Amt Bückeburg > 5.3 Einlieger und Häuslinge**. That is the one I would pursue with the archive before ordering anything else.
