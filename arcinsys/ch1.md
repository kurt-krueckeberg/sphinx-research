# **Using ARCINSYS for Genealogical Research**

## **A Practical Handbook for the Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv and the Former Principality of Schaumburg-Lippe**

### Chapter 1 – Introduction

## Who This Handbook Is For

This handbook is written for genealogists—not archivists.

If your ancestors lived in the former Principality of Schaumburg-Lippe or elsewhere in Lower Saxony, you've probably discovered that many important records are not found in church books or civil registers. Instead, they are preserved in archives and described in **ARCINSYS**, the archival information system used by the Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv and several other German state archives.

Many genealogists find ARCINSYS intimidating because it is not organized like Ancestry, FamilySearch, or Archion. It was designed for archivists and historians, not for family historians. Once you understand how archives are organized, however, ARCINSYS becomes a powerful research tool.

This handbook explains how to use ARCINSYS effectively from a genealogist's perspective.

---

## What Is ARCINSYS?

**ARCINSYS** (Archiv-Informationssystem) is an online catalog of archival holdings.

It is **not** a genealogy database.

Its purpose is to tell you:

* what records exist,
* which archive holds them,
* how those records are organized,
* whether they have been digitized,
* and how to access them.

Think of ARCINSYS as the card catalog of a very large historical library. It tells you where records are located; it usually does not interpret them for you.

---

## The Most Important Principle

The single most important concept in archival research is this:

> **Archives organize records by the office that created them—not by the people mentioned in them.**

Everything else in ARCINSYS follows from this principle.

For example, suppose Johann Heinrich Krückeberg sold his **Stätte** in Berenbusch in 1849.

A genealogist might expect to search for:

* Krückeberg
* Berenbusch
* Stätte No. 10

Sometimes that works.

More often, however, the records are found because they were created by government offices such as:

* the Amt Bückeburg,
* the Rentkammer,
* a court,
* or another administrative authority.

The archive preserves each office's records together because that reflects how the government originally conducted its business.

Once you begin thinking in terms of **which office created the document**, rather than **which person appears in it**, your success rate in ARCINSYS increases dramatically.

---

## A Real Example

Suppose your ancestor emigrated from Schaumburg-Lippe in 1849.

Instead of finding one file labeled with his name, you might discover several separate files:

* a petition requesting permission to sell his holding,
* a court record approving the sale,
* a Kaufbrief,
* Rentkammer approval,
* creditor proceedings,
* an emigration permission,
* tax settlement records.

Each was created by a different office.

Each may be located in a different archival series.

Together they tell a much richer story than any single record could.

## How to Use This Handbook

This handbook assumes no prior archival experience.

We'll begin with the basic concepts that every genealogist needs to understand before using ARCINSYS. Then we'll build toward advanced techniques using real examples from the former Principality of Schaumburg-Lippe.

By the end, you should be able to:

* understand how archives are organized,
* navigate ARCINSYS confidently,
* locate records beyond simple keyword searches,
* interpret archival references and signatures,
* and identify additional records that many researchers overlook.
