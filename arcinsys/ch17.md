# How to Find Schaumburg-Lippe Records in ARCINSYS

## 1. Search the place—not merely the surname

Begin with the village:

* Berenbusch
* Evesen
* Petzen
* Nordholz

ARCINSYS usually catalogs files by their subject or location, not every person named inside them. A file titled **“Vermessung der Dörfer Evesen, Berenbusch und Nordholz”** contains no surname in its title, for example. ([Arcinsys][1])

## 2. Filter the results to the Bückeburg archive

Select:

**Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv, Abteilung Bückeburg**

Then open promising results and click **“Kontext anzeigen”** or **Navigator**. This shows the file’s position within the archival hierarchy.

## 3. Browse the containing Bestand

Do not examine only the individual result. Open its parent Bestand and inspect:

* the Bestand description;
* the internal headings;
* neighboring files;
* other files under the same subject heading.

This is often how you discover records whose catalog titles do not contain your village or ancestor.

## 4. Start with these major Bestände

### NLA BU L 2 — Alte Regierungsregistratur, 1553–1823

Use this for older central-government records: administration, land matters, surveys, military affairs, jurisdiction and many other governmental subjects. Its alphabetical filing system can place similar subjects under different headings, so browsing several plausible headings is necessary. The archive specifically identifies **L 101a, L 20, K 1, K 2 and F 2** as important related collections. ([Arcinsys][2])

### NLA BU L 3 — Neuere Regierungsregistratur, 1701–1932

Use this for the broad range of later Schaumburg-Lippe government administration, particularly after the newer registry was established following Count Wilhelm’s death in 1777. ([Arcinsys][3])

### NLA BU L 101a — Amt Bückeburg and Amt Arensburg

Use this for local administration affecting villages within those Amts: property, petitions, local disputes, policing, poor relief and other matters handled below the central government. L 2 itself names L 101a as an important parallel Bestand. ([Arcinsys][2])

### NLA BU K 2 — Fürstlich Schaumburg-Lippische Hofkammer: Jüngeres Kammerarchiv, 1777–1918

Use this especially for **Bauerngüter, Kolonate, Stätten, Kammer property, dues and landholding administration**. It contains records of the Hofkammer’s management of income from princely and chamber estates. ([Arcinsys][4])

### NLA BU F 2 — Fürstliches Hausarchiv

Check this when L 2, L 3, K 1 or K 2 appears incomplete. F 2 contains government and chamber records that remained in the princely archive because the separation of state and household records was imperfect. It is therefore a genuine parallel collection, not merely private family material. ([Arcinsys][5])

## 5. Choose Bestände according to the question

| Your question                                   | Search first                                |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| History or transfer of a Stätte                 | K 2, then L 101a and L 3                    |
| Village administration before about 1823        | L 2, then L 101a                            |
| Nineteenth-century government matter            | L 3                                         |
| Survey, boundaries or village land              | L 2 and K 2                                 |
| Sale before emigration                          | K 2, L 101a, L 3 and emigration headings    |
| Court dispute or inheritance                    | L 20 and the applicable local-court Bestand |
| Apparent gap in government or Hofkammer records | F 2                                         |

## 6. Follow every cross-reference

When a catalog description or document mentions:

* another office;
* another Signatur;
* approval by the Regierung or Rentkammer;
* a Kaufbrief;
* a court proceeding;

search that office’s Bestand separately. The same transaction may appear in several collections.

## 7. Save the complete archival reference

Record:

> **NLA BU + Bestand + internal letter or series + file number**

For example:

> **NLA BU L 2 L Nr. 27/13**

The repeated **L** is meaningful: the first belongs to the Bestand **L 2**; the second identifies an internal filing group—in this example, records concerning *Landesvermessung*. ([Arcinsys][1])

## The essential rule

**Search by village to obtain an entry point; then browse the Bestand and its related Bestände.**

A surname search finds only records whose catalog descriptions happen to name the person. Browsing the relevant government and Hofkammer collections is how you find the records in which the person is named only inside the file.
