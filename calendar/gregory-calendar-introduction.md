# Introducation of Gregorian Calendar in Petzen Church Registers

Schaumburg-Lippe and its Lutheran state church did not adopt the Gregorian
calendar that we use until the year 1700, when February 18 was followed by
March 1, omitting February 19 through February 28.

Because the calendar had been introduced by Pope Gregory XIII, Protestant
territories preferred to call it the “Improved Calendar” rather than the
“Gregorian calendar.”

Dates of baptisms, marriages, confirmations, deaths, and burials recorded
in the Petzen church registers from 1641 through February 18, 1700, follow
the Julian calendar and are ten days earlier than the corresponding
Gregorian dates.

What does this imply? Suppose someone was baptized on June 1, 1670, and
died on June 1, 1730.

Because the baptism date was recorded under the old calendar and the death
date under the new calendar, he would not be exactly 60 years old. The old
June 1 corresponds to June 11 on the new calendar, so he died 10 days
before his 60th birthday.

His age would therefore be about 59 years, 11 months, and 20 days.

To calculate the age correctly, convert the pre-1700 date by adding ten
days before comparing it with the later date.
